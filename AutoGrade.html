Term Work Assessment

Automated Term Work Assessment System

A smart, parameter-driven system for objective, transparent, and bias-free evaluation of student term work — spanning attendance, unit tests, achievements, and mock practicals.

Core Parameters

Four Pillars of Assessment

Each parameter is independently scored and weighted to produce a holistic, fair evaluation of a student's term-long performance.

📅

Daily Attendance

Tracks presence over all scheduled lectures, practicals, and tutorials. Consistent attendance signals engagement and discipline. Calculated as (days present ÷ total working days) × 100.

Weight in final score  ·  25 marks
📝

Unit Test / Prelim

Aggregates marks from all unit tests and the preliminary examination. Best-of-N policies or average formulas are configurable per department norms to reflect true academic performance.

Weight in final score  ·  40 marks
🏆

Student Achievements

Rewards co-curricular and extracurricular accomplishments — hackathons, paper publications, sports, NSS/NCC, cultural events. Bonus marks are capped and tiered by achievement level.

Weight in final score  ·  20 marks
🔬

Mock Practical

Simulates the final practical viva. Evaluated on experiment performance, lab record, timely submissions, and oral viva responses. Prepares students while contributing to term work marks.

Weight in final score  ·  15 marks

Process

How It Works

A simple three-step pipeline from raw data to final term work marks.

01

Data Collection

Attendance is auto-fetched from biometric or LMS records. Unit test marks are imported from the examination portal. Achievement proofs are uploaded by students. Mock practical scores are entered by the examiner.

02

Normalisation & Weighting

Each parameter is scaled to a common 0–100 basis. The weighted formula is applied automatically, and edge cases (absence, bonus caps) are handled by configurable rules defined by the faculty or HOD.

03

Report Generation

A detailed PDF report is generated per student, including sub-scores, grade, improvement suggestions, and a comparison with class averages. Reports are auto-sent to students and stored for department records.
